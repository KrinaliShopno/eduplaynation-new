About Us

Home // About Us

Our Mission

Elephanta Education empowers K–12 learners to understand how they connect to the world—past, present, and future—through immersive, inquiry-driven experiences that blend emotion, cognition, and historical context. We design inclusive, rigorous learning tools that align with Science of Reading principles, strengthen vocabulary and knowledge, and spark enduring curiosity. Our mission is to equip every child with the confidence and skills needed to thrive as informed, compassionate citizens.

Our Vision

Learning science and innovative design are important, but our vision reaches further—beyond traditional learning checklists. We envision a future where learners feel safe heading into the world with joy and confidence, knowing their brightest days are still ahead.

Our programming accomplishes this by modeling how to separate and integrate affect and cognition, creating pathways that honor everyone's humanity as communities work together to transform challenges into opportunities. All while having fun.

Our Story

A journey of creativity, courage, and child-centered innovation.

Founding and Early Development (2018–2020)

Elephanta Education, L3C, was established in 2018 by social entrepreneur Victoria Sanders with a simple yet powerful goal: to help students understand their connection to the world around them. Our educational journey began in Wyoming with a board game exploring how Westward Expansion unfolded across the Northern Plains.

Over two years, we tested and refined the Bozeman Trail game with nearly 2,000 students across a dozen schools statewide, including the Wind River Reservation. While teachers were initially skeptical, they quickly recognized the game's remarkable ability to draw all learners into the story—an uncommon hero's journey that engaged every student.

Expansion During the Pandemic (2020–2021)

During the pandemic, founder Victoria Sanders used the same design template to create additional board games, layering learning standards to explore more regions and historical eras. This led to the development of BRAVE games—four long-form board games that use the same board surface to explore multiple viewpoints of the past, putting history into living, breathing context.

eduPLAYnation (2022–Present)

After the pandemic, teachers requested more time-sensitive materials that still provided skilled learning opportunities. We responded by creating JAMs: tightly-scoped, inquiry-led activities and ELA extensions that support BRAVE games. These stand-alone, short-form games proved easy to use while providing the depth and breadth teachers needed. Next, we built eduPLAYnation, an online platform that scales the magic of game-based learning to give every K4-5 classroom access to engaging, rigorous, and inclusive programming. We also formalized our commitment to innovation and social impact by becoming an L3C—a hybrid business entity dedicated to serving the greater good.

Addressing Critical Educational Challenges

Recent research has revealed serious shortcomings in the K-12 "balanced reading" approach, which isn't consistent with brain science or learning science. For the past three decades, our education system has failed to equip students with the vocabulary and knowledge needed to function as healthy, informed citizens. The statistics are sobering: according to NAEP(National Assessment of Educational Progress) data, 8th grade test scores measuring basic proficiencies have declined dramatically, with only 13% proficient in history and 20% in civics. Reading scores have suffered similarly across the board.

Our Response:eduPLAYnation aligns its programming with Science of Reading instructional guidelines, placing core content knowledge at the forefront of learning. This approach helps students reduce cognitive load in equivalent classes at higher grades, building a stronger foundation for future success.
